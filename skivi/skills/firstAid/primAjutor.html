What is Cardio-Respiratory Arrest?

Cardio-respiratory arrest can occur anytime, anywhere and especially in anyone. Every minute after the installation of the cardio-respiratory arrest, if first aid measures are not intervened, the chances of survival of the victim decrease by about 10%. The chances of life of the victim increase by about 50-70% if the resuscitation and automatic defibrillation maneuvers are performed in the first 3-5 minutes after the installation of the stop. In order to help a victim who has suffered a cardio-respiratory arrest, you must follow these steps:

HEART MASSAGE (30 external chest compressions)

1 Make sure the victim is on a hard surface.

2 Expose the victim's chest.

3 Divide the victim's sternum into two equal halves.

4 Place the bridge of the dominant palm in the lower half of the sternum.

5 Place the other hand on the sternum.

6 The elbows should be extended and the line of the rescuers' shoulders should be parallel to the line of the victim's sternum.
