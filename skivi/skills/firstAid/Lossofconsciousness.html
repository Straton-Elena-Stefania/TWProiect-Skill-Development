What is the state of consciousness, but the state of loss of consciousness?

Consciousness designates a particular cortical state, characterized by a special sensitivity, individual, to internal or external stimuli, marking an awareness of oneself and the environment. In case of loss of consciousness, the disorder can be long-term or short-term, depending on the cause. A short episode of loss of consciousness, also known as fainting, consists of loss of control over mental and physical functions. Disorders of consciousness may differ depending on the quantitative and qualitative nature. In the case of quantitative disturbances of consciousness, the state of consciousness is more and more restricted - in the case of states of confusion or drowsiness, the person in question is sleepy but not unconscious.

How are episodes of loss of consciousness formed?

How are episodes of loss of consciousness formed? Due to the complex nerve connections, the brain tolerates few deviations from the normal metabolic functioning state. To maintain this constant state, the blood supply to the brain, cranial metabolism and cranial pressure form a very sensitive control system, which immediately detects changes and reacts. When a disorder is installed in the system, all the superior cranial functions - including awareness of all aspects involved, protective functions and vital reflexes such as regulating breathing, heart rate or ensuring blood sugar levels : The brain delays the moment when survival is no longer certain and when the consequences consist of irreparable damage to the brain and body.

How do I apply first aid if a person loses consciousness?

The patient who is unconscious but breathing is at risk of obstruction of the upper airways with his own tongue or vomiting secretions / fluids if sitting on his back. Under these conditions, the unconscious victim who is breathing and not suspected of trauma will be placed in the safe side position. The following steps must be taken to place the victim in the safe side position:

1 SAFETY OF THE SAVIOR AND THE VICTIM

2 Make sure you are safe before intervening (watch out for dogs, fallen wires).

3 Make sure the victim is safe.

4 ESTABLISHING THE LEVEL OF CONSCIOUSNESS

5 Approach the victim and without touching him, ask him if he is feeling well.

6 If he doesn't answer, shake his shoulders slightly, ask him again if he's feeling well.

7 SHOUT FOR HELP

8 Call someone to come to you.

9 If necessary, he will call 112 and go for the automatic defibrillator, if you know that there is such a device in the area.

10 OPEN THE UPPER AIRWAYS

11 Place your palm on the victim's forehead.

12 Place two fingers on the victim's chin (bone).

13 At the same time, turn your head back and raise the victim's chin (hyperextension of the head with the lifting of the chin).

14 Check the oral cavity.

15 BREATHING VERIFICATION

16 Keep the airways open.

17 Sit with your ear and cheek over the victim's mouth and nose.

18 Your eyes will look at the victim's chest.

19 At the same time, you will notice if the victim is breathing for a maximum of 10 seconds.

20 If the victim is breathing, he / she will be placed in the safe side position and 112 will be contacted.

The lateral safety position of the victim will be achieved as follows:

1 Place the victim's arm closest to you at a right angle to the body with the forearm bent upwards.

2 Pass the victim's other arm over your chest and place the back of your palm on your cheek. Hold the victim's palm with your palm and then lift the victim's knee opposite you until the sole has firm contact with the ground.

3 Place your hand on the victim's thigh and turn towards you. Make sure the victim is leaning on his elbow and knee.

4 Open the victim's upper airway.

5 Reassess the victim's breathing every two minutes.

6 At 15-30 minutes, if the specialized medical help does not arrive, place the victim in a safe lateral position on the opposite side.

About the project

To design a modular Web application with the role of virtual instructor to offer sets of activities and training resources aimed at learning / deepening skills such as wire dancing, dead languages (eg, Latin, Aramaic), self-defense styles, singing to a musical instrument (harpsichord, Teremin, tuba), survival (in the jungle, in the home, cosmic vehicle, polluted neighborhood, ...), resuscitation procedures, making origami and others. Each skill will be implemented by an independent Web microservice that will be updated automatically or upon request. At least 3 such microservices will be developed as case studies. The basic functionalities of the system (instruction management, users interested in learning skills, etc.) will be offered via an API adopting the REST or GraphQL paradigm.
