Introduction

If so far you have not been passionate about cooking - or you did not have to, or the issue of food preparation did not interest you - over time you understand the need for a healthy diet, which begins, without a doubt, in your kitchen.

1. Equip your "work area" accordingly!

The work area is, you guessed it - the kitchen. And when we talk about the kitchen equipment, we refer to the choice of some accessories, equipment and working tools useful to learn how to cook properly. So, choose a set of knives so you can slice and chop the vegetables, meat and cheeses, the equipment you need most (kitchen scale, blender, juicer, food processor), pots for boiling food quantities on which you know you eat, bowls for salads and pasta, trays for sweets. For example , if you prefer cream soups, a blender is indispensable, and if you intend to cook a larger amount of soup, you will need a suitable pot.

2. Follow the preparation recommendations.

In order for a recipe to succeed, you will need to read it in its entirety, write down the necessary ingredients and make sure you have them all before you start cooking. In order to learn how to prepare delicious meals, we recommend that you follow the recipe recommendations as faithfully as possible at least at the beginning:

1 make sure you have all the ingredients purchased, in the weight provided by the recipe;

2 respects the order of boiling, chopping, seasoning;

3 opt for the recipes you like, with your favorite foods.

Always invest in quality ingredients, these are the ones that give the prepared food the perfect taste. Also, take your time and research the drinks that "match" each food: pasta, steaks, cheeses. We assure you that the culinary experience will be significantly improved.

3. Learn and respect the cooking time for certain foods.

When you start cooking, even cooking pasta proves to be a real challenge. And if we are talking about meat, it is necessary to know the optimal preparation time for each category of meat - poultry, pork, beef, but also depending on how you prefer it (more bloody or better made). It should not be extremely difficult, especially if you have a quality grill or a state-of-the-art oven, easy to adjust according to the instructions in the recipe.

Cooking with steam!

It is by far the best healthy cooking technique, and the advantage is that almost all foods, from meat to vegetables, can be steamed. The ingredients are gradually heated due to the hot steam obtained by boiling a quantity of water, and the process is a milder one, so that the food retains its nutritional value. You can cook countless steamed dishes: vegetables, meat, fish, to which you can add spices and spices as you like. And the operation is not even complicated, because there are steam cookers that quickly prepare a healthy and delicious food.

üçö

Boiling!

Boiling is a process that you need to pay close attention to, because food is prepared at a temperature above 100 degrees Celsius, which can lead, in some cases (such as vegetables), to the loss of substances. nutritious. To preserve their qualities, choose the method of simmering (even if it takes a little longer) or try to reduce the cooking time. Cooking by boiling is very simple: you only need a saucepan, ingredients for your preparation, water and salt. You have to keep an eye on the water to get to the boiling point, then add the food. In the case of some vegetables, such as carrots or zucchini, boiling is the best method of cooking: the heat enhances their taste and preserves their nutrients.

ü•ì

Grill / grill cooking!

The grill is an excellent alternative to frying food: you get a similar taste, but the nutritional intake is richer, because you do not subject the ingredients to the temperature of the frying and you do not use oil. When grilling meat or vegetables, be careful that they do not come into direct contact with the fire or smoke from the coals, as a black crust will form which may turn into a burn. These food burns are a source of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, substances that are very harmful to the body.

ü•ò

Sauteed!

Also an alternative to traditional food frying, sauteing is a technique by which the ingredients are continuously mixed in the pan with a spatula, they do not have to be left at one point to fry. In some cases, the pan is removed from the heat and the vegetables are thrown into the air. Sauteing also involves the use of oil and the heating of food in the pan, but the amount of oil used differs, as does the time in which the ingredients are cooked.

ü•ê

Baking!

In addition to being one of the simplest methods of cooking, it is also a healthy technique, as it involves preparing food at lower temperatures, a uniform distribution of heat, but also a higher retention of vitamins and nutrients. In addition, in general, baked goods do not require the addition of oil, but rather a little water, so as not to dry the ingredients. And the great advantage is that you can prepare anything in the oven (meat, vegetables, pasta, desserts) and you don't have to invest too much in this process: put the tray in the oven and just check the evolution of the preparation from time to time.
